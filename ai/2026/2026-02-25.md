## Additional Discussion
- Ariel’s proposal prompted a suggestion from Victor to discuss, within the scope of the use cases, what is being captured and for what purpose: https://github.com/aibom-squad/SBOM-for-AI-Use-Cases?tab=readme-ov-file
- Victor identified the following benefits:
  - Improved reproducibility: captures detailed metadata that enables consistent replication of model experiments and faster validation cycles
  - Transparent model development: provides a clear record of datasets, configurations, and training steps, supporting internal reviews and stakeholder confidence
  - Lineage and provenance tracking: establishes traceable links across model versions, supporting audit readiness and regulatory compliance
  - Streamlined collaboration: centralizes model documentation in a standardized format, making it easier for teams to collaborate and transfer knowledge
  - Lifecycle oversight: enables continuous tracking of models from experimentation through deployment, supporting asset inventory and lifecycle management
- Ariel responded that lifecycle oversight makes sense, and that lineage and provenance tracking could also be argued.
- A follow-up question asked which benefit applied if it was not already listed, and whether there was a new benefit.
- The answer given was lineage and provenance tracking: traceable links across model versions that support audit readiness and regulatory compliance.
- It was noted that the SHA should be the same, but it might not be, for example if a guardrail is added.
- It was observed that the base components of OpenClaw are basically the same as any other agent: LLM plus tools plus memory.
- A scope discussion followed. One point raised was that SBOM is about documenting how a product or service is manufactured or assembled.
- It was noted that process and operation can be captured as long as they relate to the creation, delivery, and deployment of the product or service.
- Concern was raised that operation after deployment may not be part of SBOM.
- It was also noted that what is new is how many tools are connected at once.
- The OpenClaw question was characterized as very much after deployment.
- Art noted that SBOM can be an input to a system architecture map, but because the system is live and SBOM is a snapshot, SBOM alone cannot serve as the map.

## Crosswalk Discussion
- “Crosswalk” was discussed as a way to compare similar but different terms from different standards.
- It is intended for ontology integration.
- IEEE, ISO, and CoSine were identified as the most complicated examples.
- It was suggested that “contains” may be used in addition to crosswalk, though how this would be expressed remains open.
- A question was raised about where this is planned to be used.
- Simple Standard for Sharing Ontological Mappings (SSSOM) may be used instead of a crosswalk.
